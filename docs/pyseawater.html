pyseawater.seawater([(float)salinity=0.035[, (float)density=1.0]]) → Medium :¶

Seawater

Construct seawater as a propagation medium given the practical salinity of the ocean site of interest.

Only the six most important (by reference salinity) materials dissolved in Standard Seawater are taken into account, according to [Millero2008]. These materials are sodium, magnesium, calcium, potassium, chlorine, and sulfate ions and will be referred to as salt.

After correcting for the missing materials dissolved in Standard Seawater via renormalization, the element mass fractions of salt are scaled by

\[S_{R} = S_{P} \times 35.16504/35\]

where \(S_{P}\) is the practical salinity of the ocean site of interest, and combined with \(1 - S_{R}\) of pure water.

Millero2008

F. J. Millero et al., Deep Sea Research Part I: Oceanographic Research Papers 55.1 (2008), pp. 50-72.

Parameters

  • salinity (float, optional) – Practical salinity

  • density (float, optional) – Mass density in g/cm3

Returns

  • Medium – Seawater

  • C++ signature – seawater::Medium seawater([ double=0.035 [,double=1.0]])
